Introduction

Elijah Price holding up a comic book.

Unbreakable is a 2000 film by M. Night Shyamalan, starring Bruce Willis and Samuel L. Jackson. It is a psychological thriller that explores the concept of superheroes and villains in a realistic setting. The film follows David Dunn, a security guard who survives a train crash without any injuries, and Elijah Price, a comic book enthusiast who suffers from a rare bone disease. Elijah believes that David is a superhuman who is unbreakable, and tries to convince him of his destiny. The film is the first installment of the Unbreakable trilogy, which also includes Split and Glass.


Cast

From the left, Bruce Willis and Samuel L. Jackson in a scene in the movie.

The cast of Unbreakable includes Bruce Willis as David Dunn, a security guard who survives a train crash without any injuries, and Samuel L. Jackson as Elijah Price, a comic book enthusiast who suffers from a rare bone disease. Robin Wright plays Audrey Dunn, David's wife and a physical therapist. Spencer Treat Clark plays Joseph Dunn, David's son. Charlayne Woodard plays Elijah's mother. Other cast members include Eamonn Walker as Dr. Mathison, Leslie Stefanson as Kelly, Johnny Hiram Jamison as Elijah at age 13, and Michaelia Carroll as the babysitter.


Plot

David Dunn wearing a poncho in the rain.

David Dunn, a security guard, survives a train crash without any injuries, while everyone else dies. Elijah Price, a comic book enthusiast who suffers from a rare bone disease, contacts David and tells him that he is a superhuman who is unbreakable. Elijah tries to convince David of his destiny and helps him discover his powers, such as enhanced strength and intuition. David becomes a vigilante and saves people from crimes and accidents. Elijah reveals that he orchestrated the train crash and other disasters to find David, and that he is a supervillain who is David's opposite. He calls himself Mr. Glass. David reports Elijah to the authorities and leaves him in an institution for the criminally insane.


Conclusion

Unbreakable is a movie that examines the realistic and psychological aspects of superheroes and villains. It tells the story of a security guard who survives a train crash without any injuries, and a comic book enthusiast who suffers from a rare bone disease. The movie challenges the audience to question the nature of destiny and the contrast between strength and weakness. It also showcases the impressive performances of the cast and the subtle direction of the filmmaker. Unbreakable is a movie that will keep you intrigued and make you wonder what is possible and what is not. It is a movie that you will never regret watching.
